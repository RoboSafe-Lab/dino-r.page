DINO-R: Explainable Out-of-Distribution Detection for Autonomous Vehicles

A lightweight, interpretable OOD detection framework leveraging frozen DINOv3 features and Monte-Carlo masking to produce pixel-level anomaly maps in real driving scenes.

GitHub Results Video Demo

Abstract

Reliable deployment of autonomous vehicles requires detecting out-of-distribution (OOD) scenarios — road conditions, objects, or weather events not seen during training — before they cause safety failures. DINO-R addresses this with a two-stage approach: frozen DINOv3 patch tokens encode rich scene semantics, while a small trainable transformer reconstructor is trained exclusively on in-distribution (ID) driving scenes. At inference, Monte-Carlo masking produces stable pixel-level anomaly heatmaps, surfacing exactly which image regions drive the OOD decision. Evaluated against 10 competing methods on the SegmentMeIfYouCan benchmark, DINO-R achieves AUROC 0.9945 and FPR@95 0.0302 — state-of-the-art among both supervised and unsupervised approaches.

Key Highlights

⚡

Lightweight Architecture

Frozen DINOv3 patch tokens paired with a minimal trainable transformer reconstructor. No large fine-tuning required.

🔒

Unsupervised ID Training

Learns purely from in-distribution driving scenes. No OOD data is ever needed for training or threshold calibration.

🗺️

High Interpretability

Monte-Carlo masking generates pixel-level anomaly maps, showing exactly why a frame is flagged as OOD.
